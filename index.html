About the Project

Building evidence for better care

SKRBI is a multi-year research initiative dedicated to evaluating and improving the quality of long-term care across Slovenia. We work with care facilities, policymakers, healthcare professionals, and citizens to build a comprehensive evidence base.

Our research spans policy analysis, care standards assessment, workforce development, and patient outcomes — producing actionable documents that drive meaningful reform in Slovenia's care infrastructure.

Policy Analysis

Systematic review of care policies and regulatory frameworks across the nation.

Data & Outcomes

Measuring care quality through evidence-based outcome metrics.

Workforce Studies

Research on caregiver training, retention, and working conditions.

Best Practices

Documenting and sharing proven approaches from leading care institutions.

Common Questions

Find answers about our research, methodology, and how to get involved.

SKRBI is a four-year research initiative (2024–2027) examining the full spectrum of long-term care quality in Slovenia. Our research covers policy frameworks, care standards, workforce capacity, patient outcomes, legislative analysis, and best practice documentation. We collaborate with 12 institutional partners including universities, care facilities, and government bodies.
New documents are published on a weekly basis. These include research papers, policy briefs, statistical reports, and case studies. You can subscribe to our newsletter to receive notifications when new documents are added, or check the knowledge base directly — documents are sorted by publication date by default.
Yes. All documents in our knowledge base are published under a Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 (CC BY 4.0) license unless otherwise noted. You are free to share, adapt, and build upon our research for any purpose, provided you give appropriate credit to the SKRBI project and the individual authors.
We welcome collaboration from researchers, care professionals, policymakers, and institutions. You can submit research proposals, participate in our data collection programmes, or offer peer review for upcoming publications. Contact us through the form on this page or reach out directly to our research coordination office.
The project is funded through a combination of Slovenian Research Agency (ARRS) grants, EU Horizon Europe programme contributions, and institutional support from participating universities. Our funding structure ensures complete editorial independence — no funder has influence over our research findings or publications.
Anonymized research datasets are available upon request for academic and policy research purposes. Individual documents can be downloaded directly from the knowledge base. For bulk data access or API integration, please contact our data management team with details of your intended use.
